Punk

Punk rock, an ofshoot of popular hard rock, emerged in the mid-1970s. Punk music strayed from the excesses of mainstream rock in the 20th century. Also known as garage rock, punk rock is often self-produced and hard hitting. Click here if you love fast-paced songs with loud hard hitting vocals, stripped down instrumentals, and sticking it to the man.

Jazz

Jazz music, a genre that orginated in African American communities in New Orleans, stems from popular folk genres, blues and ragtime. Characterized by its pulsating and rhythmic patterns, it stimulates productivity because our minds mimic the improvisation of the soulfull tunes. Click here if you love smooth rhythmic progressions, neural stimulation, and soulfull melodies.

Hip Hop

Hip hop music, also referred to as rap, developed in the United States by inner-city African Americans and Latino Americans in the late 20th century. It is characterized by its two parts: MCing and DJing. This combination of rapping and producing has had a lasting social effect leading into the 21st century. Click here if you love booming 808's, catchy beats and samples, and fiery bars.
